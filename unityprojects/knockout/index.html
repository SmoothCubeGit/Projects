Devlog: Knockout - Multiplayer Battle Royale FPS

Overview

Knockout is a multiplayer battle royale first-person shooter designed to provide a simple yet functional experience. The primary focus was on creating a multiplayer backend system alongside several core gameplay elements to outline the basic structure of a battle royale game. This project was completed within a month, and it served as an opportunity to learn and implement foundational systems for such games.

Features and Systems Created

Multiplayer Backend

  • Hosting: Developed using Photon Unity Networking (PUN) for reliable multiplayer hosting and synchronization.
  • Lobby System: Implemented a lobby system where players can join, wait, and start matches together.

Environment

  • Island Design:
    • Used Unity's Terrain Tool to manually sculpt an island.
    • Populated the terrain with trees and grass using a nature pack from the Unity Asset Store.
  • Custom Models:
    • Created a custom plane model in Blender to serve as the battle bus.
    • Designed a player character model in Blender.
    • Used a gun model sourced from the Unity Asset Store.

Audio and Visual Effects

  • 3D Audio: Added walking sound effects with spatial audio to enhance immersion.
  • Circle System:
    • Procedural scaling to dynamically reduce the playable area over time.
    • Integrated dynamic lighting to visually emphasize the shrinking zone.
    • Contact Shader: Added a white edge contour to highlight the circle boundary.

Player Systems

  • State Synchronization: Synced player states such as moving, skydiving, and parachuting across all clients.
  • Movement:
    • Procedural gunfire animation for realistic shooting.
    • Procedural head bob for a natural walking effect.
    • Stamina system to manage sprinting and energy usage.
  • Health and Gun Systems: Integrated health mechanics and damage calculation to support combat interactions.

Core Mechanics

  • Battle Bus and Spawn System: Created a system where players drop from the plane and transition smoothly into the game world via skydiving and parachuting.
  • Circle System: Designed a procedural shrinking zone to guide player movement and intensify gameplay toward the end of the match.

Development Highlights

Most Satisfying Achievements

  1. Circle System: The dynamic scaling and visual effects of the shrinking zone had a significant impact on gameplay, making it one of the most rewarding features to implement.
  2. Player Dropping System: The parachuting and skydiving mechanics added a polished and immersive entry point to each match, which was a complex yet fulfilling system to develop.

Challenges and Improvements

  1. Performance Optimization:
    • Frame rate issues arose due to high triangle counts and inefficient asset management.
    • Future improvements could focus on better triangle management and optimized rendering techniques.
  2. Asset Placement: Manually populating the terrain with trees and grass was time-consuming; procedural placement tools could streamline this process in future iterations.

Lessons Learned

This project provided invaluable experience in:

  • Building a multiplayer infrastructure and synchronizing player states.
  • Designing and implementing core game mechanics, such as shrinking play zones and dynamic spawning systems.
  • Using procedural animations and effects to enhance visual and gameplay quality.

Conclusion

Knockout successfully met its goal of creating a basic yet functional battle royale experience. While there is room for optimization and improvement, the project served as a significant learning experience in game development, particularly in multiplayer systems and complex gameplay mechanics.
